<br> #### Final Project (one that ended in failure) Unfortuantely I do not have a video of my final project because it never worked. What I do have is an explination of the intended purpose with a couple photos to accompany: My project was intended to be an automatic touniquette for quick, easy, and light application unlike the big, bulky, and hard to use ones that hospitals have. My plan was to set up a device in which a pressure sensor would be connected and as the belt tightened the pressure sensor would stop at certain points depending on the severity and location of the wound. For example: a hand getting cut off would only require between 7-9 Pounds of pressure whereas a leg would be 10 to 12PSI. Here is the blueprint I made for the way the device would look and function: ![Picture of Blueprint](./touniquete.png) The first thing I did was set up the circutry for my project. I mapped it out on a bluebook and then started my connection to a Protoboard. After about 6-7hours of soldering and planning, I ended up with this: ![Picture of Board](./Circut_board.png) After my circutry was all set up I started to design the enclosure for the board and the motors. In an insaneley unfortunate turn of events, after waiting 10 hours for my chassis to print, I found that my walls were supports and there was no time to reprint. Now I had to resort to my second option: Wood. I did not want to use wood because I knew it would be harder to connect the pieces together and it would be even harder than that to find a method at which I could effectibvely connect them so they were able to support the pressure that would (hopefully) be applied. Also, I nowe had to create a fully new sketch for my chassis so that I could use a laser cutter because that was the mose accurate and efficient way to go about using wood. Even when using the Laser cutter tradgedy struck and several of my pieces were not fully cut through. In short, the chassis building process was a disaster.
